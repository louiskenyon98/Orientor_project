Great and important question. You're trying to evaluate how well an **LLM-based semantic matcher** performs at aligning students with jobs — **but without ground truth mappings or labeled targets**. This is common in recommender systems and semantic search contexts, especially in early-stage products. Let’s walk through a set of **robust, research-backed and practical strategies** to evaluate your system **without explicit labels**: --- ## ✅ 1. **Intrinsic Evaluation: Semantic Matching Quality** Use *semantic consistency* measures to assess how "meaningful" or "coherent" the matches are. ### a. **Vector Similarity Distribution** - Calculate cosine similarity between: - a **user and their top-N job matches** - a **user and random job roles** - Plot histograms — good matches should cluster at high similarity, random ones should be near zero. - This tests the **discriminative power** of your embedding model. > **Goal**: Show that top matches are not random; they differ significantly from random pairings. --- ## ✅ 2. **Qualitative Human-in-the-Loop Evaluation** LLM outputs are best assessed **with human judgment**, especially in an exploratory career guidance context. ### a. **User Studies / Annotation** - Sample 50–100 user-job match pairs. - Ask annotators (your team, students, experts): - *Does this job seem appropriate for this user?* - *Rate this match from 1 to 5 based on relevance.* - *What part of the user’s profile supports this match?* Then compute: - **Mean Opinion Score (MOS)** - **Standard deviation** (to check agreement) - **Precision@K** if annotators flag "good" vs. "bad" matches. --- ## ✅ 3. **Contrastive Judgments / A-B Testing** Use **relative judgment** — much easier to assess than absolute accuracy. > *Which of these two jobs is a better fit for this student?* You can test: - Different embedding models (MiniLM vs. BGE vs. fine-tuned BERT) - Different vector constructions (e.g., story only vs. story + interests) This is ideal for: - **Early testing** before investing in fine-tuning - **UX design**: present two jobs and ask the user to “swipe” or pick one → feed this as future feedback --- ## ✅ 4. **Clustering Coherence & Skill Alignment** Once embeddings are computed: - Cluster job vectors using KMeans or UMAP - Visualize with t-SNE or PCA - Validate that jobs of similar *skill requirements* or *titles* cluster together > Similarly, cluster **user profiles** and test if similar users map to similar job types. --- ## ✅ 5. **Proxy Task: Self-consistency of Output** Run *ablation tests*: - Does removing parts of the user profile (e.g. hobbies, story) significantly change match rankings? - Do profiles with high “creativity” align to creative jobs more consistently? --- ## ✅ 6. **Cold Start Prompt Evaluation (LLM-on-LLM)** You can use **LLMs to evaluate your LLM system**, with carefully crafted meta-prompts. ```text Given this user profile and this job description, rate the relevance from 1 to 5. Explain your reasoning. ``` Use GPT-4 or Claude to generate a judgment and rationale. Not perfect, but provides: - Scalable annotation - Test of logical coherence --- ## 🔁 Future: Feedback Loop for Supervised Learning If you later collect: - Which jobs were clicked on - Which matches were bookmarked, skipped, or explored - Student feedback on fit You can use this to: - Fine-tune your embedding model (e.g., contrastive learning) - Train a **scorer** model on top of embedding similarity --- ## 📌 Summary: Choose Based on Stage | Approach | Best For | |---------------------------------|------------------------------------------| | Similarity distribution | Quick check on vector model performance | | Human rating (1–5) | Ground-truth proxy | | A-B preference tests | Model comparison, UX tuning | | Skill-cluster coherence | Diagnostic analysis | | LLM evaluator prompt | Meta-evaluation without users | --- Would you like help setting up one of these — say, a notebook for similarity histograms and clustering, or an annotation interface for 1–5 match rating?
